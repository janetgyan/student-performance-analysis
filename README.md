## Customer Purchase Behavior Analysis Across Demographics and Product Categories

## Overview

This project examines how various lifestyle and academic factors influence student exam performance. The analysis identifies patterns between study habits, sleep, extracurricular activities, and exam outcomes to support evidence-based recommendations for academic success.

## Objectives

* Analyze the relationship between lifestyle factors (sleep, study time, social activities) and exam scores
* Identify academic factors (attendance, study methods, course load) impacting performance
* Examine differences in performance across gender and age groups
* Provide actionable insights through dashboards for educators and students

## Dataset

* Student survey and academic performance data
* Includes demographics (age group, gender), product categories, payment method, and sales values
* Data was cleaned and prepared for analysis

## Tools Used

* Power BI (data modeling & dashboard creation)
* Excel (data cleaning and preparation)

## Key Insights

* Students who dedicate consistent study hours and maintain sufficient sleep generally perform better
* Participation in extracurricular activities has a moderate effect on exam performance
* Performance varies slightly across age and gender groups
* Attendance and engagement in classes strongly correlate with higher exam scores
  
## Visualizations

The Power BI dashboard includes:

* Exam scores by study hours and sleep patterns
* Performance comparison across gender and age groups
* Impact of extracurricular activities on scores
* Attendance vs. exam score analysis

## Conclusion

The analysis highlights key lifestyle and academic factors that influence student performance, offering actionable insights for improving study habits, time management, and overall academic outcomes.
